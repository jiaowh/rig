# M2 result — RIG inverse+active-loop vs warm-started BO, in-silico (2026-07-16)

**Milestone (implementation-plan §15.3 M2):** *does the RIG per-query inverse + cost-cooled
active-learning loop reach a target spec more cheaply than warm-started GP-EI
Bayesian optimization, in-silico?* Scored by **difference-in-RMST** (Uno 2014) on
cost-to-target survival data (event = spec hit, censor = budget-exhausted,
**smaller RMST is cheaper**).

> **Scope (binding):** this is an **in-silico machinery proof on the WP-B
> InSilicoMachine**. It is NOT evidence of a real-tool advantage — the real
> headline stays gated on **M0** (securing a real recipe→outcome dataset, still
> open). See §"Scope & honest limitations".

## Verdict

**RIG's inverse-guided active loop reaches spec significantly more cheaply than a
fixed-pool warm-started GP-EI baseline**, on a *noisy, metrology-anchored*
in-silico test, and the advantage is **robust across spec tightness** (not an
artifact of a single tolerance knob).

> **Two claims narrowed by the v2 re-validation (read the crux test + that section
> before quoting this verdict):** (1) The headline `hit 1.00 vs 0.42` compared RIG
> to a **fixed-pool** GP-EI. Giving BO a **continuous acquisition optimizer**
> (`optimize_acqf`-style L-BFGS on EI) roughly **doubles its hit-rate (0.45 →
> 0.90)** — so the *reliability* gap was largely a discretization artifact. **But
> RIG still reaches spec far cheaper than the continuous BO** (ΔRMST = −17,850,
> p=1.8e-31; RIG ~14k vs continuous-BO ~31k median cost). So the **cost-to-target
> verdict survives a competent BO**; the "100% vs 42% hit-rate" contrast does not.
> (2) The win is delivered by the inverse's **margin-guided `nearest_achievable`
> point** — the pessimistic **feasibility guarantee never binds at this spec
> tightness** (6σ) — so read the *cost* win as "the inverse-guided loop is
> cheaper", NOT "the §8 robustness guarantee is what beats BO". **IF-1 is now
> fully resolved** (see *Attribution* below): an ablation shows pessimism adds only
> a marginal, non-significant edge over a plain mean-inverse *on M2 cost*; and a
> separate binding study shows the §8 guarantee **does bind and delivers calibrated
> feasibility (0% vs 7.8% false-accept) once the spec loosens past ≈15σ** — a
> property orthogonal to the M2 cost race.

|---|---|---|
| RMST (cost-to-target, ↓ cheaper) | **15,250** | 40,780 |
| hit-rate within budget | **1.00** | 0.42 |

### The decisive evidence: the tol-sensitivity curve

| spec half-width | RIG hit | BO hit | ΔRMST |
|---|---|---|---|
| 2σ (tight) | 0.96 | 0.08 | −16,670 |
| 3σ | 1.00 | 0.12 | −23,540 |
| 4σ | 1.00 | 0.21 | −23,750 |
| 6σ (headline) | 1.00 | 0.46 | −21,250 |
| 8σ (loose) | 1.00 | 0.79 | −15,000 |

RIG wins at **every** tolerance. As the spec loosens, BO's hit-rate climbs
0.08 → 0.79 and RIG's cost advantage smoothly shrinks. This is the load-bearing
contrast with the refuted v1 (below), where the win *evaporated to a tie under a
2× tolerance change*. **Interpretation (corrected by re-validation, finding
BF-1a):** the mechanism is that RIG's inverse **optimizes recipes continuously**
into the box while BO can only pick from a **discrete 128-point pool** — and the
spec box is smaller than the pool spacing (0/128 pool points are ever in-spec), so
BO cannot *resolve* the box and its ~0.42 hit-rate is largely near-box pool points
that noise tips in-spec, not GP-EI convergence. That continuous-vs-discrete search
asymmetry — not a pessimistic robustness margin (which never binds here) — is what
the curve is really showing. A continuous-acquisition BO would search like RIG;
whether it closes the gap is the open test (§"v2 re-validation").

## Why there are two versions (the v1 refutation)

A first configuration (**v1**, `docs/m2-result.json` history) reported an even
larger RIG win. A 6-lens adversarial validation workflow (14 attackers × verify)
**refuted it**: 9 attacks survived, one **fatal**. v1's win was an artifact of a
rigged-easy setup, not a method difference:

| v1 finding | verdict | what was wrong |
|---|---|---|
| **TS1** | CONFIRMED, **fatal** | spec tolerance was a free `tol_frac·span` knob sitting in the exact window that manufactured the win; **doubling it → exact tie** |
| **A3** | CONFIRMED, major | the "coupled" target was a **separable identity** (`thickness_grown ≡ film_thickness`) — the easiest possible geometry for a direct inverse |
| A1/A2/TS2 | plausible | **all pathologies OFF** → deterministic machine; seeds weren't real realizations; the §12.1 exploitation mode never exercised |

The v1 *statistical machinery* was sound (11 stats-lens attacks correctly refuted:
horizon a-priori, censoring clean, pooling honest, bootstrap correct). The problem
was the **experimental configuration**. The honest conclusion at that point was:
*v1 is not trustworthy* — so it was not reported as a result.

### The BO baseline bug we found and fixed (BF-1)

`WarmStartedBO._distance_to_box` computed a **raw** L2 distance to the spec box.
With outputs spanning orders of magnitude in SI (`T_center` ~1e3 K vs
`bow_cooldown_um` ~1e-4 m), the scalarization was numerically **blind to the
small-scale output** — BO nailed T_center and could never satisfy the tight bow
box. That is a scalarization artifact, not a method weakness. Fixed by
**normalizing each residual by its per-output tolerance** (the standard
multi-spec scalarization). BO's hit-rate rose 0.38 → 0.58 in the smoke and it is a
genuine comparator in the powered run (hit-rate 0.08→0.79 across the tol curve).
This makes BO **stronger**, i.e. the comparison harder for RIG — and RIG still
wins. (Tests: `tests/test_warm_bo.py` unaffected; full suite 308 green.)

### Attribution — the win is the *inverse-guided* loop (with an important caveat)

Instrumented attribution (`scratchpad/attribute_rig.py`, real loop subclassed):
**RIG's hits are 100% from the inverse-solver exploit** (position 0 of the lot);
0% from the space-filling explore picks, 0% from the seed DoE. So the win is the
inverse channel, not luck or exploration.

**But (re-validation finding IF-1, CONFIRMED — now fully investigated):** the
pessimistic solver returns **INFEASIBLE on 100% of solves** at the M2 spec
tightness, and every hit is the `nearest_achievable` *fallback* point — the
κ/z_epi/δ **feasibility guarantee never fires here**. Solves now carry an explicit
attribution (`verdict ∈ {FEASIBLE_CERTIFIED, INFEASIBLE_FALLBACK}` +
distance-to-feasible + §8.8 cause) in `_inverse_readout`, so the write-up can never
again conflate the two.

*Corrected mechanism* (the earlier "aleatoric σ collapses to its 1e-9 floor →
credited band vanishes" story was diagnosed and **overturned** —
`scratchpad/if1_diagnose.py`). The real cause is two honest facts at the tight 6σ
joint spec: **(a)** the sparse GP mean *barely reaches the coupled box* — at n=8,
**0% of grid recipes put both KPI means in-box** — and **(b)** the robust margin is
standardized by a near-floor `σ_ale(bow)≈7.5e-9`, so a modest *absolute* epistemic
band (~0.5·tol) reads as a large deficit *in σ-units*; the solver's own cause
string is "mean reaches spec, uncertainty too large — collect more runs." So
INFEASIBLE is the solver being **honest**, not a degenerate test.

**Two follow-up studies close IF-1:**

1. **Ablation — is the *pessimism* what wins M2, or just the inverse?**
   (`scratchpad/if1_ablation.py`, 4 targets × 15 seeds, CRN-paired, RIG `z_epi=1,
   κ=1` vs a plain mean-inverse `z_epi=0, κ=0` in the *same* loop.) Both hit 1.00 at
   median cost 14,000; **ΔRMST[pessimistic − mean] = −1,083, p = 0.053**, differing
   in 30/60 campaigns. So the robust margin *does* reshape the fallback exploit in
   half the campaigns and is directionally cheaper, but the edge is **marginal and
   not significant**. **The M2 cost win is "inverse-guided loop beats BO," robust to
   turning pessimism off** — the mean-inverse alone (RMST 16,500) still crushes
   continuous BO (RMST 33,267).

2. **Binding study — where *does* §8 bind, and does it pay?**
   (`scratchpad/if1_binding.py`.) Sweeping seed-count × spec-looseness, the FEASIBLE
   certificate fraction is a clean step function of **spec tolerance, not data
   budget**: **0% at 6σ, 100% at ≥15σ, uniformly across n_seed ∈ {8,20,40,80}.**
   Where it binds, the pessimistic certificate delivers its intended
   **calibrated feasibility** — re-measuring each certified recipe 200× under
   metrology noise:

   | binding regime | cert | false-success (noisy accept fails) | credited-interval coverage |
   |---|---|---|---|
   | tol=15σ, n=8 (near boundary) | **pessimistic** | **0.0%** | **94.6%** |
   | tol=15σ, n=8 | mean-in-box | 7.8% | 0.0% |
   | tol=30σ, n=40 | pessimistic | 0.0% | 98.2% |
   | tol=30σ, n=40 | mean-in-box | 0.0% | 0.0% |

   Near the binding boundary the pessimistic cert admits **0% out-of-spec recipes vs
   the mean-in-box cert's 7.8%**, and its credited interval covers ≈95–98% of noisy
   re-measurements (≈ nominal) where the mean cert's degenerate interval covers 0%.
   As the box loosens, both reach 0% false-accept (nothing can fail), but only the
   pessimistic interval stays calibrated.

**Net honest attribution.** The M2 *cost* win = **inverse-guided active loop beats
BO** (not the robustness guarantee, which is inoperative at 6σ). §8's distinct value
— **calibrated feasibility** (0% vs 7.8% false-accept, ~95% interval coverage) — is
real but shows up on a **different axis** that binds at looser specs, and is
validated in-silico here. The M2 cost numbers are unchanged and the comparison
remains fair.

## Scope & honest limitations

- **In-silico only.** This proves the *machinery* on the WP-B physics stand-in
  under realistic metrology noise. Real-tool claims are **M0-gated**.
- **Triangular, not strong, coupling.** The 2-D MBE recipe space (`T_heater`,
  `film_thickness`) does not offer a strong Pareto trade-off; `T_center × bow` is
  the strongest genuine 2→2 coupling available (bow's 17% `T_heater` dependence).
  We state this rather than overclaim.
- **Partial pathology surface, and it flatters RIG (quantified, finding A1-1).**
  Only `metrology_noise` (zero-mean, RIG-benign) is ON. Adding the §10 **seasoning
  drift** — the surrogate-staleness pathology the single-shot exploit is most
  vulnerable to — degrades RIG from **hit 1.00 → ~0.71** and the margin from
  ΔRMST ≈ −22k → **≈ −14k** (full §10 surface ≈ −11.5k, still significant,
  p ≈ 7e-3). RIG still wins under drift, but the headline 100%/−25.5k figures are
  the benign-pathology best case. A full-pathology companion run is owed.
- **BO baseline is a *fixed-pool* GP-EI (findings BF-1a major, BF-1b minor).** The
  scale-fix (below) made the scalarization fair, but BO still (i) searches only a
  **discrete 128-point pool** with no continuous acquisition optimizer, and (ii)
  scalarizes the 2-KPI conjunctive spec into one GP. The canonical strong GP-tier
  baselines — continuous `optimize_acqf`/TuRBO and constrained-BO (per-KPI GPs +
  feasibility acquisition, SCBO) — are **not yet built**; they are WP-E (BoTorch,
  torch-blocked). Until then the claim is "beats a fixed-pool scalarized GP-EI",
  not "beats a competent continuous/constrained BO".
- **"6σ" is effectively ~5.1–5.8σ (finding TS1-v2).** σ is anchored at one midpoint
  recipe; the noise is heteroscedastic, so the per-target effective multiple is
  5.1–5.8σ on the binding output. Mildly flatters RIG, but **bounded by the
  tol-curve** (bracketed by the k=4/k=6 points, both decisive wins) — cannot flip
  the verdict. Per-target σ anchoring would make it a true uniform 6σ.
- **The both-hit ΔRMST is collider-conditioned (finding SV-3, cosmetic).** It
  restricts to cells where both methods hit (= exactly BO's 67 hits, since RIG hits
  all 160). The selection bias runs *toward BO* (conservative for RIG), so it
  cannot inflate the headline; the unconditional paired win-rate (0.93) is the
  companion.

### The BF-1a crux test — RIG vs a *continuous-acquisition* BO (resolved)

BF-1a was the load-bearing finding, so it was tested directly rather than
caveated. `WarmStartedBO` gained a continuous acquisition optimizer
(`acq_optimize=True`: L-BFGS multistart on the EI surface, the `optimize_acqf`
pattern, `src/rig/baselines/warm_bo.py`; default-off, suite unaffected). Three
arms on the honest config (metrology noise, coupled target, tol_k=6), CRN-paired,
4 targets × 15 seeds = 60 pairs/arm (`scratchpad/bo_continuous_crux.py`):

| arm | hit-rate | median cost (hits) | RMST |
|---|---|---|---|
| RIG | 1.00 | 14,000 | 15,417 |
| BO fixed-pool | 0.45 | 29,000 | 40,084 |
| **BO continuous** | **0.90** | 31,000 | 33,267 |

- ΔRMST[rig − bo_fixed] = −24,667 (p=6e-54); ΔRMST[rig − **bo_cont**] = **−17,850**
  (p=1.8e-31); ΔRMST[bo_cont − bo_fixed] = −6,817 (p=1.6e-3).
- **Conclusion:** continuous acquisition roughly **doubles BO's hit-rate**
  (0.45 → 0.90) — the discrete pool was a genuine reliability handicap, exactly as
  BF-1a claimed. **But RIG still reaches spec ~2× cheaper than the continuous BO**
  (14k vs 31k median; ΔRMST −17,850, ~72% of the fixed-pool gap retained), because
  its inverse converges in the *first* adaptive batch while EI-BO needs many. The
  M2 **cost-to-target** verdict survives a competent continuous BO; the reliability
  contrast ("100% vs 42%") does not and has been dropped.

**Net (post-crux):** the honest claim is *"RIG's inverse-guided active loop reaches
a noisy, metrology-anchored spec ~2× more cheaply than a warm-started GP-EI BO
(fixed-pool OR continuous-acquisition), in-silico, because its continuous inverse
converges in one adaptive batch; robust across spec tightness."* **IF-1 is now
resolved** — a feasibility-attribution readout (`_inverse_readout` verdict),
a pessimism-vs-mean-inverse ablation, and a binding study jointly show the M2 cost
win is the *inverse-guided loop* (pessimism adds a marginal, non-sig edge), while
the §8 guarantee's distinct payoff — *calibrated feasibility* — binds and is
validated at looser specs (≥15σ); see *Attribution*. Still owed for the *fullest*
comparator claim: a **constrained-BO** (SCBO) baseline (BF-1b) and a real
BoTorch/TuRBO slate (WP-E). Full finding text + verifier reasoning: workflow
`wf_c7e68a82-1c6` journal; crux run: `scratchpad/bo_continuous_crux.py`;
